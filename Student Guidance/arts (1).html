ARTS


Art is a diverse range of human activity, and resulting product, that involves creative or imaginative talent expressive of technical proficiency, beauty, emotional power, or conceptual ideas.
There is no generally agreed definition of what constitutes art, and its interpretation has varied greatly throughout history and across cultures. The three classical branches of visual art are painting, sculpture, and architecture. Theatre, dance, and other performing arts, as well as literature, music, film and other media such as interactive media, are included in a broader definition of the arts. Until the 17th century, art referred to any skill or mastery and was not differentiated from crafts or sciences. In modern usage after the 17th century, where aesthetic considerations are paramount, the fine arts are separated and distinguished from acquired skills in general, such as the decorative or applied arts. The nature of art and related concepts, such as creativity and interpretation, are explored in a branch of philosophy known as aesthetics. The resulting artworks are studied in the professional fields of art criticism and the history of arts.

Arts Courses After 12th: Top 10 Diploma Courses after 10th in Arts


  • Diploma in Fashion Designing

    • Fashion design is much more than just garments and trends; it also includes shoes, purses, footwear, and other accessories, and this course will help students establish their niche in this ever-changing business. It is a specialised course that will cover a wide range of textiles, designs, patterns, colours, and trends that, when combined, become trends. This course will assist you in developing all of the necessary design talents, such as a strong imagination, exceptional sketching abilities, and sound visualisation abilities, allowing you to obtain a job in the Fashion Design sector more easily.

  • Diploma in Beauty Culture and Hair Dressing

    • A Diploma in Beauty Culture and Hair Dressing is a one-year diploma study after the tenth grade focusing on hair and skin beauty. It is the study of topics such as hairdressing, anatomy, psychology, cosmetology, salon management, essential ICT, personal grooming, beauty techniques, yoga, health, fitness, nail structure, and so on.

  • Diploma in Photography

    • A diploma in Photography is a 1-year diploma course in the field of arts that deals with various types of professional photography like documentary photography, travel photography, sports photography, nature photography, fashion photography, etc. The course teaches students basic photography, landscape photography, commercial photography, exposure techniques, digital capturing, advanced photoshop, microphotography, flash photography, and many more.

  • Diploma in Applied Arts

    • The practical application of aesthetic and design ideas to express technical talents through creativity is known as applied arts. This course teaches students how to create functional objects while considering aesthetic and ergonomic factors. Drawing, sketching, painting, corporate communication, life drawing, advertising theory, computer graphics, and other topics are covered.

  • Diploma in Interior Designing

    • This course will teach you the fundamentals of interior design for commercial, residential, and public environments. Through internships, workshops, and projects, it will help you build a detailed approach to core concepts, techniques, material knowledge, and fundamentals. It includes topics such as Fundamentals of Interior Design, Furniture Design, Interior Drafting and Rendering, CAD, Interior Constructions, and so on.

  • Diploma in Graphic Design & Animation

    • Graphic design is an important part of all visual media. It offers classes that teach students how to use cutting-edge design tools such as Adobe Photoshop, Autodesk 3ds Max, Autodesk Maya, Autodesk, Adobe After Effects, Adobe Premiere, CAD, and others.

  • Diploma in Fine Arts

    • A diploma in fine arts is a 1-2 year program which encompasses the study of visual arts such as drawing, digital media, photography, printmaking, painting, etc. A wide range of specialisations is available in the fine arts, including video design, music composition, installation arts, painting, sculpting, etc.
